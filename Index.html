Hello, I am studying and researching Nepali folklore.

why researching Nepali folklore?

Researching Nepali folklore is crucial for preserving the nation’s rich cultural heritage and traditions. Folklore embodies the beliefs, values, and practices of communities, offering a unique window into Nepal’s history and identity. Through research, these oral and cultural treasures can be documented for future generations, ensuring they are not lost with time. Additionally, studying folklore fosters education and awareness, highlighting moral lessons, societal norms, and environmental wisdom embedded in traditional stories. It also inspires creativity in literature and art while promoting cultural tourism. Furthermore, folklore serves as a valuable resource for sociological studies, revealing insights into the dynamics of Nepali society.

Fields of Research in Folklore

The study of Nepali folklore provides deep insights into various aspects of society, culture, and traditions.

Folk Tales and Songs

Studying orally transmitted folk tales, songs, their themes, messages, and structures.

Folk Culture and Traditions

Exploring the cultural significance of traditional rituals, festivals, weddings, and other ceremonies.

Religious and Mythological Aspects

Investigating folk narratives related to deities, religious practices, and mythological events.

Sociological Studies

Analyzing the contributions of folklore to understanding societal structures, class, gender, and other social dynamics.
